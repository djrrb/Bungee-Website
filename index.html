Fonts for multi-color and vertical typography

In the crowded urban environment, space for signage is always at a premium. From dumpy liquor stores to majestic theaters, sometimes signs have nowhere to go but up. Bungee is a font family that celebrates urban signage. It wrangles the Latin alphabet to work vertically as well as horizontally; with separately glyphs, spacing, and kerning for each orientation. Bungee and comes equipped with chromatic inlines, outlines, shades, and ornaments, making it the perfect typeface to take your text in a new direction.

Tester

Colors

Chromatic typefaces have been around since the days of wood type, but the past several years have seen a renaissance of new chromatic type. Thanks to emoji, major players like Apple, Adobe, and Microsoft are coming up with color font formats, which can open up new avenues for creativity.

Bungee comes with Inline, Outline, and Shade layers, that can be mixed and matched to create a wide variety of styles. These can be implemented using common design apps, or on the web with Bungee.js.

Down & Across

Even though the Latin alphabet is meant to be writting horizontally, vertical signage is a ubiquitous element of the urban landscape. Bungee pays homage to these signs, particularly Art Deco marquees from the 1930s and 40s.

Bungee contains a specially drawn set of vertical forms, which are implemented using OpenType features more commonly associated with Asian scripts. These forms even have vertical proportional spacing and kerning.

Some design applications and browsers support true vertical text, while others require a special sideways version of Bungee that will simulate vertical type in a 90°-rotated text box.

Alts

As a display font, Bungee is only intended for small bits of text, sometimes even single letters or words. When an individual letter can play such a big part of a composition, Bungee’s alternates can help you fine-tune the look and feel of your text.

Symbols

Bungee contains arrows and pointing indexes, not to mention an extensive set of ornaments that you can use to assemble additional chromatic layers.

Development

Thanks to support from Google and The Font Bureau, Bungee released under the SIL Open Font License, which means it is free and open source.

As a libre font, I hope that Bungee will spark further experimentation in the areas of vertical and color typography.
