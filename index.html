Stack

Bungee is a typeface that celebrates the urban sign. From crummy liquor stores to majestic theaters, our cities are packed with vertical signage that stacks Latin letters, one on top of the other, in order to make dramatic use of limited space. Bungee has a specially-drawn vertical-use alphabet that will take your text in a new direction.

Setting the latin alphabet vertically is not always a good idea. The Latin alphabet is designed for horizontal writing, and readability experts would tell you to leave verticality to the writing systems that are well-suited to it, like Chinese or Korean. But in display typography, readability isn't everything. When used deftly and sparingly, stacked letters can stand out among the crowd.

It is important that each word has well-defined left and right edges, as well as a strong center line. Special care must be given to letters that are unusually wide or narrow, such as I, L, M, and W.

Bungee implements its vertical forms with OpenType features that are more commonly used for vertical text in East Asian scripts. These are activated by Vertical Type tools in some desktop design applications, or using CSS writing-mode and text-orientation.

Bungee was drawn, spaced, and kerned while rotated on its side, which led to a host of interesting design challenges. For applications that cannot take advantage of Bungee’s OpenType vertical abilities, this special Rotated version can be rotated counter-clockwise to simulate vertical text.

Color

Chromatic typefaces are nothing new: sets of wood type intended for overprinting were produced as early as 1841, and quickly became a popular way to add visual interest to posters and broadsides. Today we find ourselves in a renaissance of chromatic type. More and more digital fonts are following in the footsteps of their wooden forbears and are designed with layers for chromatic typesetting.

Bungee comes with Regular, Inline, Outline, and Shade layers, which you can mix and match to create a variety of chromatic effects. In desktop apps, you can use these layers by styling each layer on a different line and setting the leading to zero. On the web, bungee.js will do the heavy lifting for you, by arranging the text into a series of layered <div>s that you can customize in your stylesheet.

Thanks in large part to the popularity of Emoji, color font formats are now in use, with Apple, Microsoft, and Adobe offering their own specifications. I am hopeful that Bungee can help users experiment with these formats, and am excited about all of the possibilities that color fonts have to offer.

Thanks

Thanks to support from Google and The Font Bureau, Bungee is released under the SIL Open Font License, meaning that it is completely free and open-source.

I sell most of my fonts commercially, but Bungee is well-suited to a libre license because of its support for emerging typographic features like chromatic layers and vertical text. Bungee was part of Microsoft’s early experiments with color fonts, and I hope that as an open-source typeface, it will spark further experimentation with color fonts and foster further development in this field. More widespread use of color and verticality will only lead to better support for these features in desktop applications and on the web, and I hope that Bungee’s license allows it to become a valuable resource for typographers, designers, and developers (professional and amateur alike).

If you wish to support my work, please consider purchasing a license for one of my retail typefaces.
